About the Studio

Design With
Intent

Industrial design is the discipline of reducing complexity until only what is necessary remains. My practice is rooted in materials, manufacturing constraints, and the quiet intelligence of well-made things.

Based in Munich, working globally. Clients range from independent manufacturers to international hardware brands.

Disciplines

06 Areas

01

Product Design

End-to-end product development from concept to manufacturing-ready geometry. Deep familiarity with tolerances, assembly, and design for production.

02

Structural Analysis

FEA-informed design decisions. Working closely with engineers to validate stress, deflection, and failure modes at the concept stage.

03

Material Specification

Metals, polymers, composites, and bio-based materials. Material selection driven by lifecycle thinking, surface finish, and manufacturing process.

04

Rapid Prototyping

In-house FDM / SLA printing, CNC milling, and foam/cardboard modelling. Quick iteration cycles from sketch to testable form.

05

Ergonomics & HF

User research and anthropometric data integration. Products that fit the hand, body, and context of use — tested with real people, not assumptions.

06

Design Systems

Component libraries, part families, and platform thinking. Scalable design logic that reduces tooling costs and maintains aesthetic coherence across product lines.

How I Work

04 Phases
01

Research &
Discovery

Brief analysis, competitive audit, user observation, manufacturing site visits. Understanding constraints before generating options.

02

Concept
Generation

Rapid sketch ideation, morphological analysis, concept validation through low-fidelity models. Three to five concepts presented at review.

03

Development
& Detailing

CAD, material specification, tolerance definition, supplier coordination. Full 3D data ready for tooling and production engineering.

04

Validation
& Handoff

Prototype testing, ergonomic review, pre-production sign-off. Final documentation package including assembly, finishes, and BOM.
